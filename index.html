Monte Carlo Classic Problems

Exploring fundamental problems solved through random sampling

What is Monte Carlo?

Monte Carlo methods are a class of computational algorithms that use repeated random sampling to obtain numerical results. The core idea is to use randomness to solve problems that might be deterministic in principle.

Just as a casino might determine the fairness of a roulette wheel through thousands of spins, Monte Carlo methods use large numbers of random trials to estimate probabilities and solve complex problems in physics, finance, and mathematics.

π Estimation

We estimate π by comparing the ratio of points that fall inside a quarter circle to the total points in a square. Since the ratio of the areas is π/4, multiplying our ratio by 4 gives us an estimate of π.

Buffon's Needle

In this classic probability problem, we drop needles randomly on a lined surface. The probability of a needle crossing a line is related to π.

Area Estimation

We can estimate the area of complex shapes by comparing points that fall inside the shape to total points in a bounding box. This is particularly useful for shapes where traditional integration is difficult.

Integration

Monte Carlo integration estimates the area under a curve by randomly sampling points in a bounding rectangle. The ratio of points under the curve to total points, multiplied by the rectangle's area, estimates the integral.
